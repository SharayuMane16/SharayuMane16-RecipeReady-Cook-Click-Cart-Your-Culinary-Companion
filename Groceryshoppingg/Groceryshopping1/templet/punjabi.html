Sarson Ka Saag

Ingredients

▢ 750 gms sarson saag

▢ 250 gms palak saag

▢ 250 gms bathua saag

▢ 2 cups water with a pinch of salt

▢ 4 Green chilli

▢ 25 gms ginger

▢ 2 Onions

▢ 6 Garlic cloves

▢ 100 gms ghee

▢ 1/2 tsp red pepper powder

▢ 1/2 tsp garam masala

▢ 1/2 tsp coriander powder

Recipe

Step1: Add the three saags, salt and water into pressure cooker & cook over low heat for 1 1/2 hours.

Step2: Squeeze out saag and keep saag water aside. Mash saag in the cooker until coarsely ground, and add makki atta and stir.

Step3: Put back saag water and a little fresh water & boil over slow fire.

Step4: Add green chillies & ginger & cook till saag gets thick.

Step5: For tadka, add chopped onions, ginger, garlic, red pepper powder; garam masala, dhania & saute until onions are light brown.

Step6: Mix into saag & garnish with julienne of ginger fried in ghee.

Recipe

Prepare Chole:

Step1: In a pan add oil, bay leaf, cinnamon, cumin seeds, cloves, whole pepper corns, green and black cardamom.

Step2: After it gets brown add chopped onions and saute it. Now add chopped ginger and garlic

Step3: Followed by turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, asafoetida and salt, fry the ingredients together well.

Step4: For de--glazing the pan add a little water

Step5: Now add the chole (soaked overnight and pressure cooked) to the masala.

Step6: After stirring well add tomatoes, little sugar and salt to the chole

Step7: Now add ajwain, chopped green chilies and water for the base.

Step8: To get the color in the chole, add a tea bag to the masala.

Step9: Simmer the chole gently for an hour and cover it.

Step10: Add lime juice and a dollop of butter to it.

Prepare the Bhaturas:

Step1: Knead the whole wheat flour, maida and salt together with adequate amount of water.

Step2: Sprinkle the yeast on top. Leave it for 2-3 hours for the yeast to work

Step3: Divide it into equal portions. Roll out in an oval or round shape

Step4: Deep fry till golden brown, like a puri.

Step5: Serve.

Recipe

Step1: In a kadai, heat 2 tbsp butter, add 1/4 tsp jeera, 1 bay leaf

Step2: Saute, 1.5 tbsp chopped onions, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste , prepared tomato puree and saute them well

Step3: Add cooked rajma and whole urad dal

Step4: Add 1/8 tsp haldi, 1/4 tsp red chilly powder, 1/4 tsp dhania powder, 1/4 tsp salt, water as required

Step5: Boil it for 3 minutes

ButterChicken

Ingredients

▢ 400 gms raw chicken

For the Marinade:

▢ 2 tsp red chilli powder

▢ 2 tsp ginger and garlic paste

▢ 2 tsp salt

▢ 2 tsp lemon juice

▢ 1/2 cup curd

▢ 1/2 tsp garam masala

▢ 1 tsp kasuri methi

▢ 2 tsp mustard oil

For gravy:

▢ tsp oil

▢ 2 to taste butter cubes

▢ 3 gram cloves

▢ 1 Cinnamon sticks, sliced

▢ 1 tsp mace

▢ 7 Cardamom

▢ 4 Tomatoes, chopped

▢ 1 tsp garlic

▢ 2 butter cubes

▢ 1 tsp ginger garlic paste

▢ 1 1/2 tsp red chilli powder

▢ 1 tsp kasuri methi

▢ 2 tsp honey

▢ 1 green chilli

▢ 2 tsp cardamom powder

▢ 1 tbsp cream

Recipe

For marination:

Step1: In a mixing bowl, put raw chicken pieces and add salt, red chilli powder, ginger garlic paste and lemon juice. Mix well.

Step2: Refrigerate for about 15-20 minutes.

Step3: Now add curd to the refrigerated mix. Followed by salt, ginger garlic paste, red chilli powder, garam masala, kasuri methi and mustard oil. Mix well and refrigerate again for an hour.

Step4: Roast the marinated chicken in an oven for about 30 minutes until it is three-fourth done.

Prepare the chicken gravy:

Step1: Heat 2 tsp of oil in a pan with butter..

Step2: Add cloves, cinnamon stick, mace and cardamom. Saute and then add chopped tomatoes, garlic and ginger. Mix well and then grind well.

Step3: In another pan, heat another two cubes of butter, along with ginger garlic paste.

Step4: Add the tomato puree made from the mixture. Now add red chilli powder, kasuri methi, honey and finally the roasted chicken pieces. Let it simmer.

Step5: Add green chilli, cardamom powder and cream. Mix well

Step6:Serve with a teaspoon of cream over.

Tandoori

Ingredients

▢ 3 tablespoons vegetable oil

▢ 1 teaspoon ground coriander

▢ 1 teaspoon ground cumin

▢ 1 teaspoon ground turmeric

▢ 1 teaspoon cayenne

▢ 1 tablespoon garam masala

▢ 1 tablespoon sweet (not hot) paprika

▢ 1 cup plain yogurt (can sub buttermilk)

▢ 2 tablespoons lemon juice

▢ 4 garlic cloves, minced

▢ 2 tablespoons minced fresh ginger

▢ 1 teaspoon salt

▢ 4 whole chicken leg quarters (drumsticks and thighs), skinless, bone-in

Recipe

Step1: Heat the oil in a small pan over medium heat, then cook the coriander, cumin, turmeric, cayenne, garam masala and paprika, stirring often, until fragrant (approximately 2-3 minutes). Let cool completely.

Step2: Whisk the cooled spice-oil mixture into the yogurt, then mix in the lemon juice, garlic, salt and ginger.

Step3: Cut deep slashes (to the bone) in 3-4 places on the leg/thigh pieces. Just make 2-3 cuts if you are using separate drumsticks and thighs. Coat the chicken in the marinade, cover and chill for at least an hour (preferably 6 hours), no more than 8 hours.

Step4: Prepare your grill so that one side is quite hot over direct heat, the other side cooler, not over direct heat. If using charcoal, leave one side of the grill without coals, so you have a hot side and a cooler side. If you are using a gas grill, just turn on one-half of the burners. Use tongs to wipe the grill grates with a paper towel soaked in vegetable oil.

Step5: Take the chicken out of the marinade and shake off the excess. You want the chicken coated, but not gloppy.

Step6: Put the chicken pieces on the hot side of the grill and cover. Cook 2-3 minutes before checking..

Step7: Turn the chicken so it is brown (even a little bit charred) on all sides.

Step8: Move chicken to the cool side of the grill. Cover and cook for at least 20 minutes, up to 40 minutes (or longer) depending on the size of the chicken and the temperature of the grill. The chicken is done when its juices run clear.

Step9: Let it rest for at least 5 minutes before serving. It’s also great at room temperature or even cold the next day

Rajma

Ingredients

▢ 1 cup rajma

▢ 1 bay leaf

▢ 1 black cardamom

▢ 1 tsp salt

▢ 4 cup water

▢ 1 tbsp ghee / clarified butter

▢ 1 tsp cumin / jeera

▢ 1 inch cinnamon

▢ 5 cloves

▢ 1 onion (finely chopped)

▢ 1 tsp ginger garlic paste

▢ 1 chilli (slit)

▢ 2 cup tomato pulp

▢ ¼ tsp turmeric

▢ 1 tsp kashmiri red chilli powder

▢ 1 tsp coriander powder

▢ ½ tsp cumin powder

▢ ½ tsp aamchur / dry mango powder

▢ ½ tsp garam masala

▢ 1 tsp kasuri methi (crushed)

▢ 2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

Recipe

Step1: In a large kadai heat 1 tbsp ghee and saute 1 tsp cumin, 1 inch cinnamon and 5 cloves.

Step2: Add in 1 onion, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste, 1 chilli and saute until the onions turn golden brown.

Step3: Now add 2 cup tomato pulp.

Step4: Cover and cook for 10 minutes, or until the oil is separated..

Step5: Further keeping the flame on low add ¼ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp aamchur, ½ tsp garam masala and ½ tsp salt..

Step5: Saute until the spices turn aromatic.

Step6: Add in cooked rajma and mix well.

Step7: Cover and simmer for 15 minutes or until the curry thickens.

Step8: Now add 1 tsp kasuri methi, 2 tbsp coriander and mix well.

Step9: Finally, enjoy rajma with hot jeera rice.
